# 09. Trading Plan And Journal

## What Is A Trading Plan?

A trading plan is a written rulebook for trading decisions.

It helps the trader avoid emotional and random trades.

## Trading Plan Sections

### Market

Which market will you trade?

Examples:

- NIFTY 50
- Bank NIFTY
- XAU/USD
- BTC/USD

### Timeframe

Which timeframe will you use for analysis and entry?

Example:

- Daily for trend
- 15-minute for structure
- 5-minute for entry

### Setup

What exact setup will you trade?

Example:

- Breakout and retest
- Support rejection
- Trend continuation
- Range breakout

### Entry Rule

When exactly will you enter?

The rule must be clear enough that another person can understand it.

### Stop Loss Rule

Where will you exit if trade fails?

### Target Rule

Where will you book profit?

### Risk Rule

How much capital will you risk per trade?

### No-Trade Rule

When will you avoid trading?

Examples:

- After daily loss limit
- During emotional state
- During unclear market
- During high-impact news

## What Is A Trading Journal?

A trading journal is a record of every trade.

It helps identify:

- Good setups
- Bad habits
- Emotional mistakes
- Best market conditions
- Strategy performance

## Journal Fields

Every trade journal should include:

- Date
- Time
- Market
- Direction
- Entry price
- Stop loss
- Target
- Exit price
- Quantity
- Profit or loss
- Setup name
- Emotion before entry
- Mistake, if any
- Screenshot
- Lesson learned

## Process Score

Give each trade a process score from 0 to 100.

Example:

- 100: Followed all rules
- 70: Small mistake
- 40: Emotional entry
- 0: No plan

## Weekly Review

Every weekend, review:

1. Total trades
2. Win rate
3. Average risk-reward
4. Biggest mistake
5. Best setup
6. Worst setup
7. Emotional behavior
8. One improvement for next week

## Key Lesson

A trading journal converts experience into improvement. Without a journal, mistakes repeat silently.
