# 05. Trading Psychology

## Why Psychology Matters

Trading is not only chart reading. It is decision-making under pressure.

Two traders can use the same strategy. The disciplined trader can perform better because they follow rules.

## Common Emotions

### Fear

Fear appears after losses or during volatile movement.

It can make a trader:

- Exit too early
- Avoid good setups
- Move stop loss emotionally

### Greed

Greed appears after profits or during fast market moves.

It can make a trader:

- Overtrade
- Increase quantity suddenly
- Ignore exit rules

### Hope

Hope appears when a trade is losing.

It can make a trader:

- Hold a bad trade
- Remove stop loss
- Average without plan

### Revenge

Revenge trading happens when a trader tries to recover a loss immediately.

This is one of the biggest account-damaging habits.

## Discipline Rules

- Follow the trading plan.
- Do not trade without setup.
- Accept stop loss.
- Stop after daily loss limit.
- Do not increase size emotionally.
- Review every trade.

## Process Over Result

One winning trade does not mean the decision was good.
One losing trade does not mean the decision was bad.

Judge trades by process:

- Was the setup valid?
- Was the entry planned?
- Was risk controlled?
- Was the exit rule followed?

## How To Build Trading Discipline

1. Write rules before market opens.
2. Use fixed risk per trade.
3. Take screenshots of trades.
4. Maintain a journal.
5. Review mistakes weekly.
6. Reduce size after emotional mistakes.

## Signs You Should Stop Trading For The Day

- You are angry.
- You want to recover immediately.
- You entered without setup.
- You moved stop loss.
- You are trading bigger than normal.
- You cannot clearly explain why you entered.

## Practice Task

After each trade, write:

1. Emotion before entry
2. Emotion during trade
3. Emotion after exit
4. Did emotion affect the decision?
5. What should improve next time?

## Key Lesson

The market does not punish emotions. It punishes emotional decisions.
