# 01. Market Basics For Beginners

## What Is Trading?

Trading means buying and selling financial instruments to benefit from price movement. A trader may trade stocks, indices, commodities, currencies, or crypto assets.

Trading is different from investing:

- Investing usually focuses on long-term ownership.
- Trading usually focuses on shorter-term price movement.
- Investing depends more on business fundamentals.
- Trading depends more on timing, risk control, and market behavior.

## What Is A Market?

A market is a place where buyers and sellers meet. In modern trading, this usually happens through an exchange or trading platform.

Examples:

- NSE and BSE for Indian stocks and indices
- Forex market for currencies
- Commodity markets for gold, crude oil, etc.
- Crypto exchanges for digital assets

## What Moves Price?

Price moves because of demand and supply.

If buyers are stronger than sellers, price usually moves up.
If sellers are stronger than buyers, price usually moves down.

Main reasons price moves:

- News
- Economic data
- Interest rates
- Company results
- Global market sentiment
- Institutional buying or selling
- Fear and greed

## Key Words

### Bullish

Bullish means the market is expected to move up.

### Bearish

Bearish means the market is expected to move down.

### Volatility

Volatility means how fast and how much price moves. High volatility means price can move quickly in both directions.

### Liquidity

Liquidity means how easily something can be bought or sold without large price changes.

### Volume

Volume shows how much trading happened in a period. Higher volume can show stronger participation.

## Common Trading Styles

### Scalping

Very short-term trades. Usually seconds to minutes. Requires speed, discipline, and strict risk control.

### Intraday Trading

Trades opened and closed on the same day.

### Swing Trading

Trades held for a few days to weeks.

### Positional Trading

Trades held for weeks or months.

## Beginner Mistakes

- Trading without a plan
- Using large position size
- Moving stop loss again and again
- Taking revenge trades after a loss
- Following random tips
- Trading because of social media hype
- Not recording trades

## Beginner Practice Task

Open any chart and write down:

1. Is price moving up, down, or sideways?
2. Where did price recently reverse?
3. Was the move fast or slow?
4. Did volume increase during the move?
5. What would be the risk if you entered now?

## Key Lesson

Trading starts with observation. Do not rush to trade. First learn how price behaves.
