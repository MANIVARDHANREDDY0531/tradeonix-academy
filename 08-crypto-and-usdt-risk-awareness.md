# 08. Crypto And USDT Risk Awareness

## What Is Crypto?

Crypto assets are digital assets that use blockchain-based systems. Examples include Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins.

Crypto markets can run 24/7 and can be highly volatile.

## What Is USDT?

USDT is a stablecoin designed to track the value of the US dollar.

Important: Stablecoins also carry risks. Students should not assume that a stablecoin is risk-free.

## Why Crypto Moves

Crypto can move because of:

- Global risk sentiment
- Liquidity
- Exchange news
- Regulation
- Bitcoin dominance
- Large wallet movement
- Interest rates
- Social media hype

## Main Crypto Risks

- High volatility
- Exchange risk
- Wallet risk
- Regulatory uncertainty
- Scam projects
- Fake investment schemes
- Liquidity issues
- Stablecoin depeg risk

## USDT Transaction Awareness

If a business records USDT transactions, it should track:

- Client name
- Client KYC status
- Order ID
- INR amount paid
- Seller account paid to
- Bank transaction ID
- Seller USDT price
- Client USDT sell price
- USDT received from seller
- USDT transferred to client
- USDT profit
- Exact transaction date and time

## Never Teach These Claims

- Crypto is risk-free
- Stablecoins are always safe
- Guaranteed daily returns
- Double money quickly
- No loss trading

## Safer Education Language

- Understand the risk before participating.
- Learn wallet and exchange safety.
- Record every transaction carefully.
- Follow applicable law and tax rules.
- Never send money to unknown parties.

## Beginner Crypto Safety Checklist

1. Use strong passwords.
2. Enable two-factor authentication.
3. Do not share OTP or seed phrase.
4. Verify wallet address carefully.
5. Start with small learning amounts.
6. Avoid random social media tips.
7. Keep transaction records.

## Key Lesson

Crypto education must focus on risk, security, and record keeping before profit.
