# 03. Support, Resistance, And Trend

## What Is Support?

Support is an area where price has previously stopped falling and buyers became active.

It is not one exact line. It is usually a zone.

## What Is Resistance?

Resistance is an area where price has previously stopped rising and sellers became active.

Like support, it is better treated as a zone.

## How To Mark Support And Resistance

Use areas where:

- Price reversed multiple times
- Strong candles started from the level
- High volume appeared
- Price consolidated before breakout
- Previous day high or low is present

## Strong Level Vs Weak Level

A strong level:

- Has multiple reactions
- Is visible on higher timeframe
- Has strong rejection candles
- Has volume support

A weak level:

- Has only one reaction
- Is marked on a very small timeframe
- Price cuts through it often

## What Is Trend?

Trend shows the overall direction of price.

### Uptrend

Higher highs and higher lows.

### Downtrend

Lower highs and lower lows.

### Sideways Market

Price moves inside a range without clear direction.

## Market Structure

Market structure is the sequence of highs and lows.

To read structure:

1. Mark recent swing highs.
2. Mark recent swing lows.
3. Check whether price is making higher highs or lower lows.
4. Avoid assuming trend from one candle.

## Trendline

A trendline connects swing lows in an uptrend or swing highs in a downtrend.

Use trendlines as guidance, not as guaranteed support or resistance.

## Breakout

A breakout happens when price closes outside a support or resistance zone.

A good breakout usually has:

- Strong candle close
- Volume support
- Follow-through movement
- Retest or continuation

## False Breakout

A false breakout happens when price moves beyond a level but quickly returns back.

Common reasons:

- Low volume breakout
- Liquidity trap
- News spike
- Overextended market

## Beginner Rule

Do not buy directly into resistance.
Do not sell directly into support.

## Practice Task

On one chart:

1. Mark major support zones.
2. Mark major resistance zones.
3. Identify trend direction.
4. Write whether the market is trending or ranging.
5. Find one breakout and one false breakout.

## Key Lesson

Support and resistance are decision zones. A trader waits for confirmation instead of blindly entering at a line.
