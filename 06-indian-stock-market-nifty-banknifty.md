# 06. Indian Stock Market, NIFTY 50, And Bank NIFTY

## What Is The Indian Stock Market?

The Indian stock market allows people to buy and sell shares of listed companies and trade market instruments through regulated exchanges.

Main exchanges:

- NSE
- BSE

## What Is NIFTY 50?

NIFTY 50 is an index representing 50 large and actively traded Indian companies listed on NSE.

It is used to understand the broader Indian equity market direction.

## What Is Bank NIFTY?

Bank NIFTY is an index representing major banking stocks.

It is usually more volatile than NIFTY 50 because banks react strongly to:

- Interest rates
- RBI policy
- Credit growth
- Financial sector news
- Global risk sentiment

## Cash Market Vs Derivatives

### Cash Market

Buying and selling actual shares.

### Futures And Options

Contracts based on underlying assets like NIFTY, Bank NIFTY, or stocks.

Derivatives can be risky because of leverage and fast movement.

## Important Market Timings

Indian equity market regular session is generally:

- 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM

Students should always check current exchange timings and holidays from official exchange sources.

## Common NIFTY And Bank NIFTY Concepts

### Gap Up

Market opens above previous close.

### Gap Down

Market opens below previous close.

### Previous Day High

Highest price of the previous trading day.

### Previous Day Low

Lowest price of the previous trading day.

### Opening Range

High and low formed in the first part of the trading session.

## Beginner Approach

For NIFTY and Bank NIFTY, beginners should learn:

- Previous day high and low
- Support and resistance
- Trend direction
- Market breadth
- Risk per trade
- Avoiding overtrading on expiry days

## Options Warning

Options trading can be risky. Option prices move due to:

- Underlying price
- Time decay
- Volatility
- Strike price
- Expiry

Beginners should not trade options without understanding these factors.

## Practice Task

Before market opens, prepare:

1. Previous day high
2. Previous day low
3. Major support
4. Major resistance
5. Global market sentiment
6. News or events
7. Maximum loss for the day

## Key Lesson

Index trading is not about predicting every move. It is about waiting for clear structure and controlling risk.
