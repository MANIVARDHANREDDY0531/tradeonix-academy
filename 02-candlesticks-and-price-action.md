# 02. Candlesticks And Price Action

## What Is A Candlestick?

A candlestick shows price movement for a selected time period.

Each candle has:

- Open price
- High price
- Low price
- Close price

If close is above open, the candle is usually bullish.
If close is below open, the candle is usually bearish.

## Candle Body

The body shows the distance between open and close.

Large body means strong movement.
Small body means indecision or low momentum.

## Candle Wick

The wick shows rejection from high or low.

A long upper wick can show selling pressure.
A long lower wick can show buying pressure.

## Important Candle Types

### Strong Bullish Candle

Large green body with small wick. Shows buyers were strong.

### Strong Bearish Candle

Large red body with small wick. Shows sellers were strong.

### Doji

Open and close are almost the same. Shows confusion or balance between buyers and sellers.

### Pin Bar

Small body with a long wick. Shows rejection from a level.

### Engulfing Candle

A candle that fully covers the previous candle body. It can show a shift in control.

## What Is Price Action?

Price action means reading price movement directly from the chart without depending only on indicators.

Price action includes:

- Candle behavior
- Market structure
- Support and resistance
- Trend movement
- Breakout and rejection
- Momentum and volume

## Candle Context Matters

A candle pattern alone is not enough.

Example:

- A bullish candle near support can be meaningful.
- The same bullish candle in the middle of a range may be weak.

Always ask:

1. Where did this candle form?
2. Is it near support or resistance?
3. Is it with trend or against trend?
4. Is volume supporting the move?
5. What is the risk and reward?

## Common Price Action Setups

### Rejection From Support

Price falls to support, forms a long lower wick, and closes strong.

### Rejection From Resistance

Price rises to resistance, forms a long upper wick, and closes weak.

### Breakout

Price closes above resistance or below support with strong momentum.

### Retest

After breakout, price comes back to the broken level and continues in breakout direction.

## Practice Task

Take any chart and mark:

- 5 strong bullish candles
- 5 strong bearish candles
- 3 long wick candles
- 3 breakout candles
- 3 false breakout candles

## Key Lesson

Candles are not signals by themselves. Candles become useful only when combined with location, trend, volume, and risk.
