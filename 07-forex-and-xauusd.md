# 07. Forex And XAU/USD

## What Is Forex?

Forex means foreign exchange. It is the market where currencies are exchanged.

Examples:

- EUR/USD
- GBP/USD
- USD/JPY
- USD/INR

## What Is XAU/USD?

XAU/USD represents gold priced in US dollars.

XAU means gold.
USD means US dollar.

If XAU/USD rises, gold is becoming more expensive against the dollar.

## Why Gold Moves

Gold can move because of:

- US dollar strength or weakness
- Inflation expectations
- Interest rate decisions
- Central bank policy
- Global uncertainty
- War or geopolitical tension
- Bond yields

## Forex Risk

Forex can be highly volatile and risky. Leverage can increase both profit and loss.

Students must understand:

- Spread
- Leverage
- Margin
- Lot size
- Stop loss
- Slippage

## What Is Spread?

Spread is the difference between buy price and sell price.

Wider spread means higher cost.

## What Is Leverage?

Leverage allows a trader to control a larger position with smaller capital.

Example:

- With high leverage, a small price move can create large profit or large loss.

Beginners should be very careful with leverage.

## What Is Margin?

Margin is the amount required to open a leveraged trade.

If loss becomes large, the broker may close the trade or require more funds.

## XAU/USD Beginner Rules

- Avoid trading during very high-impact news until experienced.
- Use small position size.
- Never trade without stop loss.
- Check support, resistance, and trend on higher timeframe.
- Do not chase fast candles.
- Record every trade.

## High-Impact News To Watch

- US Federal Reserve decisions
- Inflation data
- Jobs data
- GDP data
- Geopolitical news
- Dollar index movement

## Practice Task

For XAU/USD, mark:

1. Daily trend
2. 4-hour support and resistance
3. Previous day high and low
4. News events for the day
5. Risk per trade

## Key Lesson

Forex and gold can move very fast. A trader must respect volatility and never trade money they cannot afford to lose.
