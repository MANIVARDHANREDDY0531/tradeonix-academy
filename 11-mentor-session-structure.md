# 11. Mentor Session Structure

This file can be used to conduct live classes, mentorship sessions, or recorded lessons.

## Session 1: Introduction To Trading

Topics:

- What is trading?
- Trading vs investing
- Risk warning
- Market types
- Beginner mistakes

Activity:

- Ask students why they want to learn trading.
- Explain that trading is skill-based, not luck-based.

## Session 2: Chart Reading

Topics:

- Candlesticks
- Timeframes
- Support and resistance
- Trend direction

Activity:

- Open a chart and mark support/resistance live.

## Session 3: Risk Management

Topics:

- Stop loss
- Position sizing
- Risk-reward
- Daily loss limit

Activity:

- Calculate quantity using sample capital and stop loss.

## Session 4: Psychology

Topics:

- Fear
- Greed
- Revenge trading
- Discipline
- Process score

Activity:

- Review emotional mistakes from sample trades.

## Session 5: Indian Market

Topics:

- NIFTY 50
- Bank NIFTY
- Previous day high/low
- Expiry-day caution
- Options risk

Activity:

- Prepare a pre-market plan.

## Session 6: Forex And Gold

Topics:

- Forex basics
- XAU/USD
- Spread, leverage, and margin
- News risk

Activity:

- Mark XAU/USD higher timeframe support/resistance.

## Session 7: Crypto And USDT Awareness

Topics:

- Crypto volatility
- Stablecoin risk
- Wallet safety
- Transaction records

Activity:

- Explain safe record keeping for USDT transactions.

## Session 8: Strategy Building

Topics:

- Setup rules
- Entry and exit
- Backtesting
- Avoiding overfitting

Activity:

- Build one sample strategy checklist.

## Session 9: Journal Review

Topics:

- Trade journal
- Process score
- Mistake tracking
- Weekly review

Activity:

- Fill one sample journal entry.

## Session 10: Final Trading Plan

Topics:

- Personal trading plan
- Risk limit
- No-trade conditions
- Improvement plan

Activity:

- Each student creates a one-page trading plan.

## Teaching Tone

Use clear, honest language:

- "This is risky."
- "Do not trade without a plan."
- "First protect capital."
- "Practice before real trading."
- "Your goal is discipline, not excitement."
